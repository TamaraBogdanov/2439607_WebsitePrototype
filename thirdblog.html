Blog Post 3 - Interaction for Data Visualisation

Interaction and data visualisation is known to go hand in hand. Whilst these are difficult terms to properly define, by looking at Dimara and Perin’s, What is Interaction for Data Visualization. I was able to get a better understanding of how the two work together and how I can use this knowledge to improve my future web development.

To start with, in order to get some impactful information, Dimara and Perin’s text was predominantly based on the research and feedback of humans and their thoughts and experiences with these terms. It also just showed me that even terms such as “interactivity” and “data visualisation” may seem so simple, it is always good to research and dive deep into the terms to better understand them, this is also a vital trait to have in the development world as research and feedback will be the basis of improving your work.

Within the text, using this feedback gathered they began to discuss how interaction and visualization worked together, and how the actions were classified as both internal and external entities and actions. I was a bit confused on what this meant so through researching I found that External entities means “any individual, organization or government body other than the applicant group.” (Law Insider) and internal actions would then be the recipients within the group.

External actions for “What Interaction for Visualization Must Involve” is about “interaction being a dialogue between the user and the visualization system” (Dimara and Perin, 2020) which is made up of actions involving input, where the visualization system then returns an output/reaction. Similarly, Internal actions suggested similar where “interaction with a visualization system involves a cognitive act of the user”. (Dimara and Perin, 2020)

When we look at Murray’s Interactive Data Visualization for the Web, he speaks about how “visualization is a process of mapping information to visuals” (Murray, 2017. pp. 1) Data visualisation plays a vital role in mapping the information in order to be read and understood by the user, thus creating an interactive output due to the input received. It “Needs to be visualized in a certain way to show interactivity” (Knaflic, 2015. Chapter 5)

Murray dives further into how humans are “intensely visual creatures” (Murray, 2017. pp. 1) and we need patterns, charts, text etc to extract powerful meaning from the information. Without data visualisation, the information becomes boring and dull and leaves no room for interactivity as the user will not only be unable to properly understand but will leave them uninterested. The only interaction you will get is them clicking off.

Knaflic discusses how accessibility, affordances and aesthetics are the most important tools in creating successful data visuals. “You need to eliminate distractions, highlight the important stud and create a clear hierarchy of information” in order to “indicate to our audience how to use and interact with our visualizations” (Knaflic, 2015. Chapter 5)

Looking into the work we have done this semester, this is why it is vital for us to use CSS correctly and nicely style, colour code and format our text, layouts and visuals in order to have successful affordances and promote interactivity. With the fairly new concept of D3.js we have been learning, this has been a perfect way to relay information in a clear manner through creating our own charts, and in future as we dive further into this it will open us up too many other of its wonders that will allow us to structure and format our data more neatly.

All of these elements of raw data need proper data visualizations not only to process them but give them meaning. We need to manipulate our data visualizations in such a way that it creates interactivity. Dimara and Perin’s text mentions the “Seven Concepts that Characterize Interaction in HCI” (Dimara and Perin, 2020) and how they work together to create interactivity. I have gone through a few and decided four out of seven resonate the most with me in defining interactivity, as well as relating to my course.

The Transmission is the interaction between the computer and human. It is based off of the “amount of error-free information per time unit that is transferred over a communication channel” (Dimara and Perin, 2020) will depend on how the user interacts with information.

We also need to remember that according to the text, interactivity can be two things, being how the user understanding the information. They interact and engage with content they understand (UX). The second being how they then physically interact with the visuals (UI) based off of the (UX). Knaflic mentions not to overcomplicate. Complexity will lead in poor interaction and therefore there will be no interactivity.

This is something we need to understand, as understanding how one interacts with information will give us control.

Control shows us how the user operates through goal driven actions” (Dimara and Perin, 2020) will. We need to understand this and understand how to manipulate our data visuals in order to minimize the error and distance between the user reaching this goal. By making our designs simpler and pleasing, our “audience will have more patience with our visuals, increasing our chance of success for getting our message across” (Knaflic, 2015. Chapter 5)

However, even though it is vital to understand how one engages with something, it is also important to understand that different people have different ways of engaging with the material.

By understanding Embodiment, we need to make our users feel as if the technology is a part of them.

Dimara and Perin also mention how “How Visualization Conceptualizes Interaction” and how we need to also create flexible interactions that pay respects to the outside world. External entities suggested how “Many modalities (e.g., gaze, head) tend to be overlooked in visualization” which leads to “disjointed picture of human performance” [79] and lost opportunities to capture user intent” (Dimara and Perin, 2020)

It is up to us to create flexible interactions in order to accommodate for this and understand embodiment. The text yet again reviewed papers in order to learn and understand how to do this. Which yet again shows how vital research is in this industry, we need to research every little bit of information so we are able to understand the best forms of creating user flexibility.

Through researching human emotions as well as how certain people with disabilities interact and think, it will allow for us to create visuals to accommodate them, allowing for total comfort when using the platform. Yet again, by doing so our visuals will work hand in hand in successfully relaying the information to the users, and in return provide us with interactivity and embodiment.

Everything mentioned thus far plays a purpose, but at the end of the day it all falls under Experience. “Human experience as a key factor in how interaction unfolds” (Dimara and Perin, 2020) We need to understand “aesthetics, emotion, fulfilment, surprise, stimulation” (Dimara and Perin, 2020) to create good interaction. Emotional usability is vital for us to understand. According to Hassenzahl and Tractinsky (2003), they argued on how “humans interacting with technology are depicted as having mostly negative emotions”.

Dimara and Perin’s text helped me realise that by creating good data visuals, we promote good interactivity with not only the content, but with the way it is engaged with and this in return promoted good user experience. However, all of this would not be possible without proper forms of research. It was evident on how Dimara and Perin’s text has used research in order to base their arguments and create solid foundation. This is what we as web developers need to strive to do as well. By researching we will be able to understand what our users want, what they think, how they interact. This will help us to create good data visualization and because of understanding what our users want, the data will be successfully interacted with by the users promoting not only mental interactivity, but also physical which as we know, deems whether our website is successful or not.
